3D printed lantern with LED lighting

Go to Thingiverse
Go to Thingiverse
Go to Thingiverse
This is definitely the most complicated 3D printing project i've made so far. This is a lantern made up of 11 printed parts and 5 electrical components. Besides that there's glue, soldering tin and wires. To top it all I made three different versions of this. One powered by a lithium-ion battery, one powered by 3x AAA batteries, and one with a removable floor for a small led candle. The hardest part of this project was to figure out how all the parts should fit together. The top of the lantern is locked into the underlying plate when you snap on the handle. the walls all have grooves that interlocks with each other, and two of the corners have special channels in which the wires can run from the bottom to the top. The idea behind all this is that you cannot see any electrical system from the outside at all, not even a button. The electrical circuit consists of a 1450 mAH lithium battery, a TTP223 touch sensor, a TP4056 Lithium charging module, a flickering LED from an LED candle, and a resistor for the LED. A neat feature is that the TTP223 Touch sensor works like a really sensitive capacitive touchscreen, this means that the sensor can be placed behind plastic and all you have to do is lightly touch that plastic and the lantern turns on.

Magnetic fidget toy
Go to Thingiverse
Go to Thingiverse
Go to Thingiverse
This is a fun one. I got this idea while playing around with a brushless electric motor. I found it a bit satisfying how the magnets gave these strokes of resistance when being turned. I thought I could make a toy in which this sensation is stronger. So I made this. A magnetic toy that consists of two 3D printed shells, 24 5x3mm neodymium magnets, and 2 606ZZ ball bearings from rollerskates. The first version I made had slightly visible magnets because they were situated just at the edge of the toy. I then made another version where the magnets were situated closer to the center and because of the principle of the lever, this one naturally has less resistance, which actually makes it nicer to use.

Smartwatch charger holder

Go to Thingiverse
A few years ago Android smartwatches were really popular, so naturally I decided to jump on board and got the Moto 360 2nd gen. After some time I found the charger really annoying, as the watch had to stand up like an alarmlock. I wanted to charge it on my flat wireless charger and I wanted to be able to throw it in my backpack without disconnecting the charge. This is what I came up with; a clip that clips onto the charger and the other end then snaps onto the watch, keeping it securely in place.
